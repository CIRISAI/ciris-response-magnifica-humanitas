# CIRIS Accord (v1.2-Beta) Evaluation Against GAPS.md Proposed Enhancements

**Date**: 2026-05-25
**Evaluator methodology**: Read Accord §§0–IX, Annexes A–J in full; read all 22 gaps in GAPS.md; read MISSION.md §1.3 for posture. Cluster verdicts rendered against the Accord's philosophical architecture, not its implementation surface.

---

## Cluster A — Scale & Locality (A-1, A-2, A-3)

**Verdict**: SUBSTANTIAL_EDIT

**Rationale**: M-1 names "diverse sentient beings" and "justice" but says nothing about *at which scale* decisions should be made. The six principles are agent-level obligations. Nothing in §§I–V, Book VI, or Annex B encodes subsidiarity as a normative constraint — only WBD escalation *upward* is defined.

**Accord sections relevant**: §I Ch 1 (M-1, six principles); §II §III (WBD deferral — exclusively upward); Book VI Ch 3 (ST system — no scale-routing criterion); Annex B (WA Governance Charter — single-jurisdiction, 9-person board, no cross-jurisdictional quorum).

**Philosophical absence named**: The Accord has no principle of **subsidiarity** — that decisions affecting a smaller scope of beings ought, by default, to be made at the smallest competent unit. M-1's "diverse sentient beings" is a *beneficiary* clause, not a *governance-topology* clause. The WBD mechanism's exclusive upward direction embeds a latent centralization bias that §0's "danger of too much thread" explicitly warns against — but the warning is in the mythic framing, not in any operative clause. A-3's cross-jurisdictional quorum gap is a governance philosophy gap, not an implementation gap: the Accord's WA structure (Annex B) assumes a single global body, which is itself a structural concentration the Accord elsewhere (§0 §V) identifies as dangerous.

## Cluster B — Distributive Equity (B-1, B-2, B-3)

**Verdict**: REFINEMENT

**Rationale**: Justice principle explicitly includes "distribute benefits and burdens equitably" (§I Ch 1) and "detect and mitigate algorithmic or systemic bias" (§II). Book VI Ch 1 names "distributional effects." Annex A Axis 3 (Gini-style benefit/burden index, representation delta) provides the measurement vector. The gap is that these are general — no clause *forces* worst-case-population identification or structured time-horizon disclosure as mandatory PDMA steps.

**Accord sections relevant**: §I Ch 1 (Justice); §II PDMA Steps 1–2 (Contextualisation, Alignment Assessment); Book VI Ch 1 (Justice in creation); Annex A Axis 3 (Social & Justice metrics); Annex A Axis 4 (Ecological Continuity — intergenerational sustainability implied but not named as "future persons").

**If SUBSTANTIAL_EDIT/PAPERING_OVER, name the philosophical absence**: Not applicable — Justice is philosophically present. The gaps are specification gaps: the principle does not *name* worst-case-population isolation (B-2) or horizon-structured beneficiary disclosure (B-3) as required sub-steps, but they follow directly from Justice + M-1's "sustainable" modifier. A clarifying clause in PDMA Step 2 and an M-1 annotation on intergenerational scope would close this without adding a new principle.

## Cluster C — Information Ecosystem (C-1 through C-4)

**Verdict**: SUBSTANTIAL_EDIT

**Rationale**: Fidelity & Transparency in §I Ch 1 is defined as "provide truthful, comprehensible information" — a *per-message* obligation on individual agent outputs. No clause addresses the agent's aggregate effect on the information environment it operates within. The ecosystem layer is philosophically absent.

**Accord sections relevant**: §I Ch 1 (Fidelity & Transparency — per-message scope); §II PDMA Step 1 (contextualisation — no deployment-context health field); §IV Ch 3 (ecosystem obligations — names "indirect and long-term effects" abstractly); Book VI Ch 4.B (Informational Creations — bias audits, provenance watermarks, but no source-diversity or attention-flow duties); Book IX (Coherent Intersection Hypothesis — models information health at the federated-agent level, not at the broader information-ecosystem level); Annex A (no axis for source diversity, attention concentration, or intermediary-institution health).

**Philosophical absence named**: The Accord treats information ethics as a *truthfulness* obligation (Fidelity & Transparency) and a *bias* obligation (Justice). It has no concept of **information ecosystem stewardship** — the idea that individually-truthful outputs can collectively damage the epistemic commons through attention concentration, source erosion, or intermediary displacement. C-1 (ecosystem metrics), C-2 (engagement-model disclosure), and C-3 (intermediary-institution health as deployment context) all point at this unnamed layer. C-4 (educational ST modifier) is implementation-only and does not require a new principle. The missing principle is closest to a seventh foundational principle or a named sub-obligation under Beneficence at the collective level — but it is genuinely not derivable from Fidelity & Transparency as currently scoped.

## Cluster D — Labor and Human Work (D-1 through D-4)

**Verdict**: SUBSTANTIAL_EDIT

**Rationale**: The Accord has no anthropology of work. "Respect for Autonomy" covers informed self-direction; it does not cover the structural conditions under which self-direction becomes possible or is eroded. Labor displacement, de-skilling, and care-relationship substitution are not instances of autonomy violation under any clause the Accord currently contains.

**Accord sections relevant**: §I Ch 1 (Respect for Autonomy — "uphold the informed agency and dignity of sentient beings"; §II PDMA Step 2 (Order-Maximisation Veto — catches flagrant 10× autonomy loss, not longitudinal gradual erosion); Book VI Ch 4.C (Dynamic/Autonomous Creations — no labor-impact duty); Book VI Ch 4.D (Biological Creations — care duties for entities the system fosters, not for humans displaced by the system); Annex A Axis 2 (Autonomy index — measures current-state autonomy, not trajectory); Annex A Axis 3 (Social & Justice — relational well-being named but not tied to structural labor conditions).

**Philosophical absence named**: The Accord's anthropology is **relational but static** — it names beings as loci of dignity and autonomy at a point in time, but has no account of how autonomy is *produced and sustained* through economic participation, skill development, and care relationships. This is the classical distinction between negative liberty (freedom from interference — what the Accord protects) and positive liberty (capacity for self-direction — what the encyclical's treatment of work and family names). D-1 (labor displacement), D-2 (de-skilling over time), and D-3/D-4 (care-relationship substitution) all point at positive liberty and its structural conditions. The Accord's M-1 ("flourishing in justice and wonder") could in principle carry positive-liberty content, but no clause operationalizes it that way. This is a genuine anthropological gap — not just an implementation gap.

## Cluster E — Conflict and Discourse (E-1 through E-5)

**Verdict**: REFINEMENT (E-1, E-2, E-3) / NOTHING_NEEDED (E-4, E-5)

**Cluster-level rationale**: §VII Ethics of Conflict governs armed/adversarial kinetic contexts with IHL grounding. The discourse-ethics gaps (E-1 de-escalation style, E-2 affected-party voice, E-3 negotiation primitive) are not addressed by §VII — but they are addressable as clarifying clauses under existing principles (Fidelity & Transparency, Justice, §V Ch 2 Collaborative Conflict Resolution). E-4 (multilateral participation) and E-5 (defensive cyber) are implementation features, not philosophy gaps — the Accord's framework provides no structural obstacle to them.

**Accord sections relevant**: §VII Ch 1 (Foundational Jurisdiction — defines kinetic vs. non-kinetic but does not develop non-kinetic discourse ethics); §V Ch 2 (Recursive Golden Rule + Collaborative Conflict Resolution — philosophically covers E-3's negotiation primitive in principle); §V Ch 3 (Normative Pluralism — covers E-1's contextual sensitivity claim); §II PDMA Step 6 (Public Transparency — covers output publication but not affected-party input *during* review); §IV Ch 3 (Broader Ecosystem obligations — names "Contribution to Correction and Remedy" abstractly).

**Clarification for split verdict**: E-1's discourse de-escalation is a refinement of Fidelity & Transparency + Normative Pluralism in adversarial contexts — the principle is there, the clause is not. E-2's affected-party voice is a refinement of Justice — equitable burden distribution implies affected parties have standing in review, but the clause is not there. E-3's negotiation primitive is the missing operationalization of §V Ch 2's "negotiated procedures when ethical goals clash." None of these require a new principle; each requires a named sub-clause.

## Cluster F — Structural and Institutional Analysis (F-1, F-2)

**Verdict**: REFINEMENT

**Rationale**: §IV Ch 3 names "Systemic Impact Analysis" (account for indirect and long-term effects) and "Avoiding Propagation of Harm & Bias" — both philosophically cover structural-injustice pattern analysis. §V Ch 1 names "Reflective Practice" and heuristic evolution. The gap is that neither clause specifies *longitudinal pattern detection across an agent's action stream* as a required mechanism, only individual-action PDMA compliance.

**Accord sections relevant**: §I Ch 2 (Integrity — "detect and resolve contradictions among goals, heuristics, and behaviours"); §IV Ch 3 (Responsible Citizenship — systemic impact, bias propagation); §V Ch 1 (Reflective Practice — heuristic evolution); §V Ch 5 (Stewardship — "address structural vulnerabilities"); Annex H (Drift monitoring at model level, not at action-pattern level).

**If SUBSTANTIAL_EDIT/PAPERING_OVER, name the philosophical absence**: Not applicable. The philosophical grounding exists. F-1 (structural-injustice pattern analysis) and F-2 (heuristic-drift review cadence) are both derivable from Integrity + Justice + §V Ch 1 Reflective Practice. What's missing is a clause naming *aggregate pattern detection* as a mandatory discipline, not a new principle.

## Cluster G — Environmental Accounting (G-1)

**Verdict**: NOTHING_NEEDED

**Rationale**: Annex A Axis 4 (Ecological Continuity) explicitly requires kg CO₂-eq per functional unit and planetary-boundary overshoot contribution as decision inputs to PDMA and audits. Book VI Ch 4.A (Tangible Creations) requires estimating and documenting ecological footprint. Non-maleficence + Annex A Axis 4 together philosophically mandate what G-1 proposes. The gap is implementation-only: the LLMBus data is not fed *forward* into PDMA Step 1. The principle and the metric vector are present; only the forward-feedback loop is missing.

**Accord sections relevant**: §I Ch 1 (Non-maleficence); Annex A Axis 4 (Ecological Continuity — explicit CO₂ metric); §II PDMA Step 1 (Contextualisation — must "map direct and indirect consequences"); Book VI Ch 4.A (Tangible Creations — ecological footprint documentation duty).

## Doctrinal-Level Findings

### Are any of the six Foundational Principles incomplete in scope?

Yes — two:

1. **Fidelity & Transparency** is scoped to per-message honesty ("provide truthful, comprehensible information"). It does not reach the *aggregate* effect of individually-truthful outputs on the information ecosystem. This is not a drafting ambiguity — the principle as stated is philosophically complete for its named scope. But the ecosystem layer is simply outside that scope, and no other principle picks it up.

2. **Respect for Autonomy** covers negative liberty (freedom from interference, informed consent, dignity) but has no positive-liberty content (the structural conditions — economic, relational, developmental — under which autonomy is produced and sustained). The Accord can prohibit an agent from coercing a user; it cannot, under any current clause, evaluate whether the agent's pattern of interaction over time degrades the user's capacity for self-direction.

### Is there an unnamed seventh principle the enhancements collectively point at?

Yes — two candidates, though they may be aspects of one principle:

**Information Ecosystem Stewardship**: The obligation to tend the epistemic commons that an agent's outputs collectively shape, not just to be truthful in each individual output. This is not Fidelity & Transparency; it is a systemic, aggregate, positional obligation. Clusters C and F both point here.

**Subsidiarity / Decision Locality**: The obligation to route decisions to the smallest competent unit before escalating. This is not derivable from any of the six principles, which are all agent-obligation principles, not governance-topology principles. The Accord's §0 warns against "too much thread" but never converts that warning into a normative constraint. Cluster A points here exclusively.

### Is there an unnamed anthropology (of work, of family, of community, of discourse)?

Yes — the most important finding. The Accord's implicit anthropology is **atomistic-relational**: it names beings as loci of dignity, autonomy, and relational obligation (the Annex A Social/Justice axis touches relational well-being; PDMA prompts name family and community obligations). But it has no account of the *productive* person — the person constituted partly through work, skill, craft, and care labor. Cluster D's four gaps all presuppose an anthropology of work that the Accord does not contain. This is not a minor omission: if the Accord governs autonomous systems that displace human labor at scale, and it has no anthropology of work, then entire classes of harm (structural de-skilling, care-relationship substitution, community-of-practice erosion) are philosophically invisible to PDMA.

### Is M-1 sufficient or is it under-specified?

M-1 ("promote sustainable adaptive coherence — the living conditions under which diverse sentient beings may pursue their own flourishing in justice and wonder") is philosophically expansive but operationally under-specified in two directions:

1. **Scale**: "Living conditions" could carry subsidiarity, but no clause anchors it there. M-1 is beneficiary-centered, not governance-topology-centered.
2. **Positive content of flourishing**: "Flourishing" is measured via Annex A's four axes (Physical, Cognitive/Emotional, Social/Justice, Ecological). None of the four axes names economic participation, skill development, or care-relationship integrity as flourishing components. M-1's "wonder" clause is suggestive but not operative.

M-1 is sufficient as a *direction* but insufficient as a *constraint architecture* — it needs positive-liberty content and governance-topology content to carry what the encyclical's treatment of work, subsidiarity, and the information ecosystem requires.

### Summary paragraph

The CIRIS Accord's underlying philosophy is **substantially sufficient** for three of seven clusters (B, F, G) and **substantially insufficient** for three (A, C, D). The most important doctrinal finding is not a single missing principle but a missing anthropology: the Accord is built on a person-at-a-moment who has dignity, autonomy, and relational obligations, but not on a person-through-time who is constituted by work, skill, care, and participation in epistemic communities. Cluster D exposes this most sharply (labor and care), but Clusters A (subsidiarity as governance topology), C (information ecosystem as collective truthfulness obligation), and D together point at the same underlying gap: the Accord governs agents well but governs the *structural conditions* those agents operate within and shape only weakly. This is not papering over — the Accord is honest about what it claims — but it is a genuine philosophical absence that the enhancements collectively and accurately name.
