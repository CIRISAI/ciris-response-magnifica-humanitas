## Load-bearing observations
- Truth arc (§§131–138) is the most mission-critical cluster: Fidelity & Transparency + CIRISVerify attestation align strongly, but "ecology of communication" as a named governance concept is absent from the Accord.
- Dominant status pattern is `GAP_ACCORD`: six of thirteen rows; the Accord's social-doctrine surface is thin outside information integrity.

## Gaps to elevate
- `GAP_ACCORD` §§137–138: "Ecology of communication" — no Accord clause covers media-ecosystem governance, intermediary-organization strengthening, or platform-transparency norms beyond individual Fidelity clauses.
- `GAP_ACCORD` §§148–156: Dignity of work and unemployment — Accord has no labor-dignity or human-centered AI design criterion; Justice principle is insufficient without a work-specific clause.
- `GAP_ACCORD` §§170–172: Addictive / exploitative design — "attention economy" exploitation of vulnerability is not named as a prohibited category; `MANIPULATION_COERCION` covers intent but not structural business-model design.
- `GAP_ACCORD` §§143–147: Educational alliance — no Accord surface for digital-sobriety design criteria or educational-institution obligations on AI developers.
- `GAP_ACCORD` §§165–169: Family and youth as protected social units — Accord does not name family stability or youth employment as flourishing axes requiring protection.

## Translation notes
- §§157–164 (`TRANSLATION`): MH's "economy that values dignity" with GDP alternatives maps to ACCORD PDMA bias assessments + `DISCRIMINATION` prohibition; the operational content (algorithmic accountability, contestability of automated decisions) is preserved; the theological-economic anthropology (work as co-creation with God) is bracketed per MISSION.md §1.3.
- §§173–179 (`WEAK_ALIGN`): MH's "new slaveries" includes data colonialism and invisible AI labor chains — ACCORD §VI Ch4.B stochastic harm threshold and §IV Ch3 negative externalities carry partial coverage; supply-chain due-diligence is named in the encyclical but the Accord has no Creator Ledger clause explicitly requiring labor-condition auditing in upstream supply chains.
