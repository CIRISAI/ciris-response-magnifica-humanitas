### T-3.A: Ecology of Communication (¬ß137 primary; see also ¬ß¬ß134‚Äì136)

**What the encyclical names**: "ecology of communication" as a system-level concept ‚Äî the set of norms, institutions, practices, and cultural formations that constitute a healthy information environment. **What the existing language covers**: `fidelity:truth_as_common_good` covers the normative grounding; `prohibited:deception_fraud` + `prohibited:manipulation_coercion` cover the NEVER_ALLOWED violations; `detection:correlated_action:informational_asymmetry:*` covers the structural-injustice detection; `dma:idma:k_eff` covers epistemic-diversity measurement. **What is not fully carried**: the *system-level* concept of a healthy information environment as a managed object ‚Äî something you can assess as more or less healthy, more or less resilient, and deliberate over as a governance goal. `goal:community` (at the decision-hierarchy level) can be given the subject of "healthy information ecology" and this is the natural locus. **T-3 verdict**: MILD ‚Äî existing primitives compose to reach most of the content; the gap is expressive (naming the system-level object) not structural. **Would survive ¬ß1.10.1 gate?** Potentially, if operationalized as: k_eff measured over a representative sample of public-discourse attestations at community or national scope, with correlation-risk thresholds calibrated per communication-context type. Technically feasible; requires RATCHET calibration package amendment for `media_ecology:information_diversity` axis.
