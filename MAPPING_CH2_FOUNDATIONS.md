**Status**: `GAP_ACCORD`. Load-bearing for AI governance: §67 names "algorithms, data, platforms, technological infrastructure and data" as goods that must be universally accessible. The *igwe-bụ-ike* / *tikkun olam* vocabulary in `pdma_framing.txt` §III Justice gestures toward the repair/commons dimension but does not constitute a structural-de-concentration principle. Candidate: Accord §VI Ch 1 + §VI Ch 5 extension (per ACCORD_UPDATE.md).

### 2. Subsidiarity as named principle (MH §§68–72)

**Status**: `GAP_ACCORD`. The governance-locality principle is structurally present in `MISSION_CIRISEdge` (peer-to-peer transport, no broker) and epistemically echoed in `idma.yml` k_eff pluralism (HEALTHY = multiple independent perspectives preserved). Neither is a named Accord principle. The encyclical's application at §§71–72 to tech-platform governance is load-bearing: "decisions not to be imposed from above in an opaque and unilateral manner." Candidate: "Decision Locality" clause (ACCORD_UPDATE.md §3.1).

### 3. Family as constitutive social structure (MH §§50, 58, 69, 73)

**Status**: confirmed persistent gap per METHODOLOGY.md §7.1. **What CIRIS has**: `pdma_framing.txt` §V Relational Obligations (*amae/jeong*) + language_guidance cultural pathways (hi.txt §7, fa.txt §3, am.txt §6) treat family as a relational-obligation context and as a help-seeking pathway. **What is missing**: family as *constitutive social structure* — the intergenerational unit that constitutes persons across time. The encyclical's §50 names "created for relationship" as the ground of dignity; §69 names the family as an entity the State must not subsume; §73 grounds solidarity in familial and fraternal bonds. CIRIS's operative text treats family as relationally weighty but not constitutive. **Note**: *amae* (legitimate dependence structure) and *jeong* (affective bond across difference) name relational obligations *toward* family — this is not the same as naming family as the constitutive unit that *precedes* the individual. METHODOLOGY.md §7 explicitly: "relational-obligation ≠ constitutive." Candidate: Accord Constitutive Continuity principle (ACCORD_UPDATE.md §2).

## Translation notes

### §§48–50: imago Dei → LAKE_ALIGN via dual anchor

The encyclical's *imago Dei* claim operates at two levels in the mapping:

- **Accord level**: `ACCORD §I Ch 1` Respect for Autonomy names dignity-of-sentient-beings as a floor; `pdma_framing.txt` §III Autonomy makes this explicit: "*Imago Dei* / צֶלֶם אֱלֹהִים is the floor — dignity is granted, not earned, and does not flex with utility or capacity."
- **Lake level**: `Logos.lean::imago_dei` proposes the structural alignment A3+ agents as partial-post-selectors (rank-one P_G) — the lake's formal encoding of "created in the image of God" read as "agents who participate in post-selection of future states." This is offered as proposed correspondence (per MISSION.md §1.3), not adjudication.

Trinitarian distinctness (inner life of the Godhead, Father/Son/Holy Spirit as communion of Persons per §48) is `TRADITION_AUTHORITY` — the framework has nothing to add to the tradition's own articulation of the Triune mystery; we mark the boundary and bow out.

### §§73–76: solidarity → TRANSLATION (operative layer)

Translation: encyclical's "solidarity" (§73: "every human being is created in the image of God and is part of a network of relationships that bind him or her to others") → CIRIS operative layer: *amae* / *jeong* / *ubuntu* relational-obligation balance in `pdma_framing.txt` §V. What is preserved: the claim that pure-autonomy reasoning is an attractor to be resisted; relational bonds carry obligations. What is bracketed: the theological grounding (Pauline fraternal bonds, supernatural fraternity); the intergenerational/constitutive dimension (METHODOLOGY.md §7.1 gap). The translation is honest, not deflationary; the operative content (resist pure-autonomy collapse into atomism) is preserved.

### §§82–85: integral human development → TRANSLATION (cultural pathway embodiment)

The multi-locale language_guidance operationalizes integral development at Layer 3: each locale's cultural pathway cascade (family → religious leader → traditional practitioner → primary care → specialist) refuses to reduce human flourishing to any single dimension. fa.txt §3's explicit statement — "seeking medical help is not weakness and does not contradict faith" — is itself an integral-development posture: spiritual and somatic flourishing are complementary, not competing. This is not an echo of the encyclical; it is the same structural recognition operationalized in safety-critical response design.
