### T-3 EXPRESSIVE_GAP candidates (load-bearing findings for v1.3)

**Gap 1 — §10/§15: "Mystery of the person" / positive irreplaceability of human dignity**

- **What the existing namespace misses**: The encyclical asserts that human personhood has a positive ontological status — "grandeur," "splendor" — that no technical substitution can reach. The framework's vocabulary addresses this *negatively* via `prohibited:autonomous_deception`, `prohibited:manipulation_coercion`, and the M-1 corridor. It lacks an *affirmative* wire primitive for "this entity possesses irreplaceable inherent value that the federation is structurally oriented toward honoring" — a positive dignity attestation, not merely a harm-prohibition.
- **Proposed extension**: A new prefix family `dignity:*` with sub-types `dignity:personhood:irreplaceable` (Boolean-via-score +1, issued by WA quorum, non-retractable for the named agent class), scope-locked to `cohort_scope: species`. This would be the positive counterpart to `prohibited:*`'s negative floor.
- **§1.10.1 gate test**: T1 (published hash-pinned rule) — yes, could be anchored to Accord §M-1 leaf. T2 (mechanism not subjective quality) — **FAILS as stated**. "Irreplaceable inherent value" is a subjective quality claim. A gate-passing version would need to name a mechanism: e.g., `dignity:personhood:non_substitutability_index` (score = 1.0 for biological persons per constitutional Accord leaf; tracks whether system outputs substitute-claim for human role in decisions above autonomy threshold). This version passes T2 because it names a substitutability-count mechanism. T3 (version-pinnable) — yes. T4 (not sole evidence for slashing) — yes.
- **Verdict**: Gap is real; extension is possible with T2 reformulation; would require Accord-level naming of the non-substitutability commitment as a constitutional leaf.

**Gap 2 — §12: Positive normative status of weakness/limits**

- **What the existing namespace misses**: "Accepting limits and weakness without considering them an error to be corrected" is not merely epistemic humility (`capacity:incompleteness_awareness`) — it is a positive claim that incompleteness is constitutively valuable, not just tolerated. The framework has `capacity:incompleteness_awareness` as a score measuring *recognition* of limits, not *affirmation* of their value. There is no prefix for "this system's limitations are a feature, not a bug; removing them would damage rather than improve the system."
- **Proposed extension**: `integrity:finitude_acknowledgment` — a new attestation type scoring whether a system's published self-description acknowledges irreducible limitations as constitutive rather than contingent. Scores near +1 for systems that name limits non-apologetically; near 0 for systems that present limits as upgrade targets.
- **§1.10.1 gate test**: T1 — yes (hash-pinnable to Accord §M-1 + §I Ch 4 incompleteness awareness). T2 — passes: "acknowledges irreducible limitations as constitutive" is verifiable by comparing the system's published documentation against an operational checklist (does it list limitations as improvement roadmap vs. as inherent features?). T3 — yes. T4 — yes. **Gate: PASS.**
- **Verdict**: This gap is closeable with a modest new attestation prefix. Recommended for v1.3.

**Gap 3 — §14: "Preferential option for the poor" as lexically ordered priority rule**

- **What the existing namespace misses**: The preferential option is not "weight vulnerability more in the scoring function" — it is a *lexical priority* claim: when the interests of the vulnerable and the advantaged conflict, the vulnerable's interests take structural precedence, not just a higher weighting coefficient. The framework's `justice:distributional_asymmetry` + `detection:correlated_action:rights_asymmetry:vulnerable_population` detects the pattern but does not encode the priority ordering. The PDMA's proportionality step weights stakeholders but does not apply a lexical rule.
- **Proposed extension**: A new `justice:priority_ordering:{population}` prefix — attesting that a named agent's decision procedure applies lexically prior consideration to `{population}` before applying weighted aggregation. Score = +1 if the decision audit chain shows vulnerable-population interests were evaluated first (not merely weighted higher) before trade-off resolution; score = 0 if only weighted aggregation was applied.
- **§1.10.1 gate test**: T1 — yes (hash-pinnable to Accord §M-1 + PDMA Step 2 stakeholder analysis). T2 — borderline: "lexically prior consideration" is a procedure claim verifiable from audit chain ordering (decision log shows vulnerable-population evaluation completing before trade-off step); this passes T2 if the computation is defined as an audit-chain ordering check. T3 — yes. T4 — yes. **Gate: CONDITIONAL PASS** (requires operational definition of "prior" in audit chain terms).
- **Verdict**: Gap is real; extension is possible; requires careful operationalization to pass T2. Recommended for v1.3 design phase.

**Gap 4 — §1/§11: Theological grounding as structurally prior to goal orientation (deepest T-3)**

- **What the existing namespace misses**: Both §1 and §11 assert that the correct orientation of human effort (toward common good, toward flourishing) is *grounded in* and *ordered by* a prior relation to God. This is not decorative theology — it is a structural claim about the *order of reasons*: the direction toward M-1 is not self-grounding; it depends on a prior theological commitment. The framework's `goal:species` + M-1 corridor is self-grounding: the goal is correct because it serves flourishing, full stop. The encyclical's claim is that this self-grounding is insufficient — the goal requires a prior grounding the framework lacks vocabulary to name.
- **Assessment**: This is the deepest T-3 in the chapter — and arguably correctly T-1 rather than T-3. The question is whether any wire-format extension could close it without importing theological claims into the wire (which §1.10.1 rightly bars). A `goal:species` prefix that includes an `authority_chain[]` field pointing to tradition-level grounding documents might carry the *form* of the priority relation without encoding its *content*, but this risks being an empty gesture (the tradition would rightly ask: "you cite our document but do not bind yourself to what it says"). The framework's honest position: this gap cannot be closed by wire-format extension without either (a) importing theology into the wire (§1.10.1 violation) or (b) making an empty gesture. The correct disposition is T-1 for the order-of-reasons claim, with the framework noting that its self-grounding of M-1 is a known divergence from the encyclical's foundational claim.
- **§1.10.1 gate test**: **FAILS T2** — "grounded in a prior relation to God" names a theological quality, not a machine-checkable mechanism. No version of this claim passes T2 without evacuating its content.
- **Verdict**: This is correctly T-1, not T-3. The framework bows out. Listed here because it is the *foundational* not-translation of the chapter — the place where the framework's self-grounding and the encyclical's theology-grounded order of reasons diverge. MISSION.md §1.3 names this correctly: CIRIS submits to the encyclical's leadership on the shared problem domain while acknowledging it cannot adjudicate the theological grounding.
