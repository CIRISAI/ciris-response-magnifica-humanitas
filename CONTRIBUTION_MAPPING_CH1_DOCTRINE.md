### Most striking clean translation

**§36 — "structures of sin"** → `detection:correlated_action:aggregate_footprint:expendability_of_persons`

Paul VI's most demanding legacy — that the Gospel must pass judgment on economic and political *structures* as a distinct moral category, not just on individual acts — is the claim the framework was specifically extended to carry. FSD-002 §3.5.3's `detection:correlated_action:{axis}` is explicitly cross-referenced to MH §36 + §§77–81 in the FSD changelog (`ciris-response-magnifica-humanitas#2`). The wire format speaks this claim cleanly: LensCore-owned, RATCHET-calibrated, WA-adjudicated, polarity-carries-verdict. The encyclical's most structurally novel insight in this chapter has a named, operational, version-pinnable translation.

### Most striking T-1 (TRADITION_AUTHORITY — papal-Magisterium claims)

**§29 — ecclesial genealogy** (Scripture → Fathers → Scholastics as authoritative sources)

This paragraph is purely the tradition's account of its own intellectual lineage as authoritative. There is no wire translation possible or appropriate: the claim is that *these sources, in this lineage, by this authority* constitute the doctrine. The framework's polyglot grounding (29 locales, cross-tradition vocabulary from pdma_framing.txt) is a parallel-but-distinct multi-tradition plurality; it is not a substitute for this self-account. The framework correctly bows out. Honest T-1: the Magisterium is the authority on its own lineage.

**Runner-up T-1 (embedded)**: §44's anchor to *Dilexit Nos* — "there is no greater way to love than concrete love for brothers and sisters as response to the Heart of Jesus." The Christological grounding of social action (love as response to the Sacred Heart) is the tradition's own claim; the framework reads the structural shape (solidarity, concrete action) but cannot adjudicate the theological ground.

### T-3 candidates flagged for v1.3

**T-3.1 — §31 / §43: "subsidiarity as named Accord principle"**
The operational shape exists comprehensively (CIRISEdge peer-to-peer no-broker, PDMA Step 5 relational balance, NodeCore "no central scorer"), but no `accord:soft_constraint:subsidiarity` named leaf exists. Per METHODOLOGY.md §7.3, this is a documented `WEAK_ALIGN` awaiting Accord-level naming. Proposed extension: add Decision Locality clause in Accord §II Ch 1, citing the implementation surfaces already present.

**T-3.2 — §43: "universal destination of goods" as normative allocation principle**
`detection:correlated_action:aggregate_footprint:*` carries the *detection* side (something is wrong with the distribution); no Accord clause carries the *normative allocation* side (goods are for all, not only the owner). This is METHODOLOGY.md §7.3's confirmed gap. The extension that would close it: `accord:soft_constraint:universal_destination_of_goods` leaf + companion Accord §VI Ch 1 / §VI Ch 5 clause, passing the §1.10.1 gate as a principle-level (not wire-level) addition.

**T-3.3 — §32 / §41: "universal moral law" / "charity united with truth as constitutive" — boundary check**
These sit at the T-1 / T-3 boundary. The framework reads them structurally (Accord constitutional-leaf immutability = functional analog of natural law; Integrity principle = functional analog of charity-in-truth). The question for v1.3: does the structural reading *carry* the moral content, or does it *point toward* it while something else carries it? The chapter-focus cue suggests checking whether the `accord:hard_constraint:*` constitutional-leaf structure is rich enough to express "this constraint is not merely procedural but grounded in the nature of persons." Current wire format is silent on the *grounding* of constitutional leaves; a `schema_ref` + hash-pinned rationale field could close this expressively.
